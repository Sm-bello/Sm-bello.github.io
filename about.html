Who I Am

An engineer who codes, researches, and ships — all at once.

I'm in the final year of my Aerospace Engineering degree at the Air Force Institute of Technology (AFIT), Kaduna, Nigeria — graduating with a CGPA of 4.13/5.00. But my undergraduate years look less like a typical student's and more like a research lab's output log.

While completing coursework in Flight Dynamics, Aerospace Structures, and Propulsion, I simultaneously authored four original research papers — all now under peer review at top international journals. I built and deployed production software. I designed a conceptual aircraft. I engineered a blockchain security system for aviation. I published open datasets used by researchers worldwide. And I did most of it independently, from a laptop in Abuja.

"I am not waiting to graduate before doing real engineering. The work is already here."

In September 2026, I begin an M.Sc. in Smart Aviation at Beihang University (BUAA), Hangzhou, China — ranked #1 in China and top-5 globally for aerospace — under Professor Hu Yang at the Smart Aviation Center. My research will focus on digital twin-based intelligent maintenance for aircraft systems.

What Drives Me

African aviation needs infrastructure it can call its own.

My SIWES internships — at the Nigerian Civil Aviation Authority (NCAA) working on airworthiness compliance, and at Executive Airlift Group maintaining King Air 350 aircraft at Nnamdi Azikiwe International Airport — gave me something textbooks cannot: a direct view of where African aviation breaks down.

The maintenance tools, the health monitoring systems, the security infrastructure — almost all of it is foreign-built, foreign-licensed, and foreign-maintained. African operators pay enormous sums for systems designed for different contexts and different fleets. I want to change that.

Every system I build — every paper I write — is aimed at that gap. The long-term vision behind Penelope Inc., the company I'm founding, is to become the leading indigenous aerospace AI engineering practice in Africa: building digital maintenance infrastructure that airlines, MROs, and regulators on this continent can actually own.

Research & Publications

Four papers. Four journals. All as an undergraduate.

  • CJA AeroTwin: B787 Landing Gear Prognostics — Three-layer edge AI with ODE physics engine, CNN-BiLSTM classifier, and offline Ollama LLM. Cranfield University collaboration. Under review at Chinese Journal of Aeronautics.
  • IEEE TAES PHI-CHAIN: Blockchain-Secured Avionics — Ed25519 + Hyperledger Fabric securing ADS-B, ACARS, CPDLC. Split-Payload Architecture resolves the TCAS Paradox. 100% attack detection rate.
  • RESS B787 Ignition Digital Twin — Physics-derived 27,000-sample dataset, LightGBM (R²=0.87), FastAPI microservice. Under review at Elsevier Reliability Engineering & System Safety.
  • AIAA JAIS Dornier 228 Certification-Compliant DT — Simscape Multibody + dual-input LSTM (98.74% accuracy), CS-23.473 compliant, 47ms latency. Under review at AIAA Journal of Aerospace Information Systems.
  • SciTech 2027 RDE CFD Augmentation via CVAE — 12 OpenFOAM simulations → 72 synthetic spatiotemporal fields (CNN-BiLSTM CVAE, 4.3M params). Novel finding: 4× CJ velocity overestimate at off-stoichiometric conditions.

I also serve as an invited peer reviewer for AIAA JAIS — on behalf of Prof. Peng Wei, George Washington University — an honour normally extended to faculty and senior researchers.
