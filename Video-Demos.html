Landing Gear Digital Twin Architecture for Predictive Maintenance and Structural Health Monitoring

Mohammed Bello Sani (Contributing Author) et al.

This paper presents a comprehensive Digital Twin architecture designed for aircraft landing gear systems, integrating physics-based modeling with real-time sensor data analytics to enable predictive maintenance capabilities and continuous structural health monitoring. The proposed architecture addresses critical challenges in aviation maintenance by creating a virtual representation that accurately mirrors the physical behavior of landing gear components under various operational scenarios.

The research demonstrates the application of digital twin technology for modeling landing impact scenarios, including normal operations, hard-landing events, and fault injection simulations. By analyzing structural response patterns and correlating them with maintenance requirements, the system provides maintenance engineers with actionable intelligence for proactive decision-making.

Results indicate significant potential for transitioning from traditional time-based maintenance schedules to condition-based maintenance strategies, offering improvements in aircraft safety, operational availability, and maintenance cost optimization.
